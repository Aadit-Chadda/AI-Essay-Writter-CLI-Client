

1. Mountainous Landscape: The Indian subcontinent has some of the world’s highest mountain ranges, including the Himalayas, the Karakoram Range, and the Vindhya Range.

2. Coastal Landscape: India’s long coastline is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, including the Andaman and Nicobar Islands and the Lakshadweep Islands.

3. Desert Landscape: The Thar Desert and the Great Rann of Kutch are two of India’s most famous desert landscapes.

4. Plateau Landscape: India is home to several plateaus, including the Deccan Plateau, the Satpura Plateau, and the Chota Nagpur Plateau.

5. Plains Landscape: India’s plains landscape is home to some of the most fertile agricultural land in the world, including the Indo-Gangetic Plain and the Great Plains of North India.

6. Forest Landscape: India is home to some of the world’s most biodiverse forests, including the Western Ghats, the Himalayan forests, and the Sunderbans.
