

The economic system of Canada is a mixed economy, which combines elements of a market economy and a command economy. Canada has a strong private sector, but the government plays an important role in deciding economic policy. The government provides services such as healthcare and education, and regulates certain industries.

The economic system of China is a Communist-Socialist system, which is based on a five-year plan. The Chinese government has a large role in the economy, and controls many industries. The government also sets prices and wages, and is involved in the allocation of resources. Private enterprise is also allowed in China, but the government tightly regulates it.
