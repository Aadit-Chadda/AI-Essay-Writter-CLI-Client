
1. Mountain scenery: The Indian subcontinent is home to some of the highest mountain ranges in the world, including the Himalayas, Karakoram and Vindhya ranges.
2. Coastal scenery: India's long coastline has some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, including the Andaman and Nicobar Islands and the Lakshad Islands.
3. Desert landscape: Thar Desert and Kachidalan are the two most famous desert landscapes in India.
4. Plateau landscape: India has several plateaus including Deccan, Satpura and Chotnagpur.
5. Plains: India's plains are home to some of the most fertile agricultural land in the world, including the Indo-Gangetic plain and the Great Plains of northern India.
6. Forest landscapes: India has some of the most biodiverse forests in the world, including the Western Ghats, Himalayan forests, and the Sandarbans.
