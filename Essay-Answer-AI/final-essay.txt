
 Canada's economic system is a mixed economy, combining elements of a market economy and a planned economy. Canada has a strong private sector, but the government plays an important role in determining economic policy. Governments provide services such as healthcare and education, and regulate certain industries.
 China's economic system is a communist socialist system based on five-year plans. The Chinese government plays an important role in the economy, controlling many industries. The government also sets prices and wages and participates in the allocation of resources. China also allows private companies, but the government strictly regulates them.
